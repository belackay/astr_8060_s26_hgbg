Science Goal: Create an RR Lyrae variable-star light curve and measure its period, amplitude, and phase using g′/r′ imaging.

Target Source: Targets will be selected from variable-star catalogs such as AAVSO VSX and verified in SIMBAD. The final target will be chosen based on brightness, period, amplitude, observability, and the availability of comparison stars.

Scientific Justification: Known variable stars are a straightforward science case for time-series photometry. The constructed light curve can be compared with catalog values and used to connect the observations to catalog classifications.

Technical Justification: Exposure time will be driven by the need to achieve photometric precision that is sufficient to detect the expected variability while sampling the light curve enough to obtain the period and phase. Needed values include target brightness, amplitude, period, exposure-time estimates, overheads, and total observing duration.

Deliverables: Light curve, phased light curve, periodogram, derived period/amplitude/phase, comparison to catalog.
